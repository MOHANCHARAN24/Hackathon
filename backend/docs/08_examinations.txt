DSIR Recognition
The Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) is a part of the Ministry of Science and Technology, which was announced through a Presidential Notification, dated January 4, 1985 (74/2/1/8 Cab.) contained in the 164th Amendment of the Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961.The Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) has a mandate to carry out the activities relating to indigenous technology promotion, development, utilisation and transfer
DSIR has been decided to accord the recognition to Vignan’s foundation of Science & Research (Deemed to be University), Guntur District, Andhra Pradesh from 30 Aug 2018 to 31 Mar 2021.

REFUND/CANCELLATION POLICY
Fee refund norms against cancellation of admissions:
For cancellation of admission, the issue of refund of tuition fee to student shall be decided in the light of MHRD circular, UGC norms (UGC regulations 2016, 6.2) and University policy. The broad rules for refund of tution fee against cancellation of seat are:
Admission fee is non-refundable, once student is provisionally admitted.
Tuition fee paid will be refunded if the student cancel his /her admission before the commencement of class work.
Prior to closing of admissions and after commencement of class work, tuition fee is refundable with proportionate deduction of monthly fee.
Once admissions are closed, the entire fee collected shall be forfeited.
ARIIA
Atal Ranking of Institutions on Innovation Achievements (ARIIA) is an initiative of Ministry of Education (MoE), Govt. of India to systematically rank all major higher educational institutions and universities in India on indicators related to “Innovation and Entrepreneurship Development” amongst students and faculties. The first edition was launched in 2019.
The methodology designed to calculate ARIIA metrics considers all major parameters/ indicators used globally to rank education institutions for innovation output. Additionally, ARIIA also measures some parameters which are India specific considering the current scenario and status of higher education in India.
Vignan Institute’s departments like Bio-tech, Food, Agricultural and Engineering Technologies s are part of innovation and startup ecosystem of the institute.

Assessment Registrations
It is mandatory for the all students to inform the institution their willingness for attending the summative assessments in the form of registering by paying a stipulated fee. The guidelines for registration are part of the assessment notification and give the details of registration fee to be paid, dates of fee payment and mode of payment. For students who are enrolled for the courses during the regular semester, assessment registration is integrated with the course registration. Students who are registering for supplementary assessments need to follow the specific registration guidelines as outlined in the assessment notification. There is no need to register for formative assessments.

Results
The assessment results are organized under three main headers, reflecting the different components of the assessment scheme. Here’s an overview of each category and the result access process:
1. Formative Assessment Marks: This section provides marks for each individual formative assessment tool, allowing students to see their performance on these specific assessments.
2. Finalized Formative Assessment Marks: This section displays the finalized marks for formative assessments, adjusted as per the relevant program’s assessment scheme.
3. Final Results: This section shows the overall result for each subject, which combines scores from both formative and summative assessments.
Release and Access to Results
• Timeline: Final results are typically released within two weeks following the last scheduled test date, as per the assessment timetable.
• Access: Students can view their results by selecting the relevant assessment link and entering their registered number.
This structured format provides transparency and a clear breakdown of students' performance in each component of their assessments.

Disciplinary Actions
In academic institutions, maintaining exam integrity is paramount. To this end, clear guidelines are established to deter malpractices. These rules prohibit possession of unauthorized materials and communication with others during exams. Violations result in expulsion from the exam hall and cancellation of subject performance. Severe cases may lead to broader consequences, including legal action. Upholding these standards ensures fairness and credibility in the evaluation process.
